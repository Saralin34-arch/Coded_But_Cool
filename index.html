Translating biological intelligence into design tools

A generative design plugin that helps researchers, architects, and engineers create efficient, resilient, and carbon-conscious structures by embedding nature’s evolutionary logics into the design process.

Designed for biomimetic workflows, early-stage exploration, and energy-aware design.

About the Plugin

This plugin bridges speculative bio-inspired research and real-world application. It turns evolutionary logics—growth, adaptation, redundancy—into controllable design parameters that can be applied to surfaces, structures, and façade systems.

Biological Intelligence

Model patterns from fungal networks, branching systems, and other organisms as parametric logics that you can tune in real time.

Design Integration

Built for computational design environments so you can plug directly into existing Grasshopper or BIM workflows.

Carbon-Conscious

Pair formal exploration with performance metrics—optimizing for material efficiency, daylight, and energy impact.

Key Features

Generative Growth Systems
Control growth radius, branching density, and iteration depth to simulate bio-inspired networks across surfaces and volumes.
Surface & Façade Mapping
Map patterns onto complex geometry, façade panels, or structural grids while keeping parameters editable.
Performance Hooks
Connect to daylight, radiation, or energy analysis tools to evaluate different growth logics against performance targets.
Export-Ready Output
Bake geometry for fabrication, documentation, or BIM integration with clean, organized layer and data structures.

How it Works

1

Install & Connect

Drop the plugin files into your Grasshopper components folder and restart. The components appear under a dedicated “Bio-Intelligence” tab.

2

Define Inputs

Select host geometry (mesh or surface), seed points, and environmental data if available (sun vectors, radiation maps, etc.).

3

Tune Growth Parameters

Adjust sliders for branching, decay, and iteration. Watch the pattern adapt in real time as you balance performance and expression.

4

Export & Iterate

Bake results, hand off to BIM or fabrication, or keep iterating as you refine your design intent.
